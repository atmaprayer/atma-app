Chapter 2, Verse 47
कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥
Karmanye Vadhikaraste Ma Phaleshu Kadachana
Ma Karma Phala Hetur Bhur Ma Te Sango Stv Akarmani
You have a right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.
Swami Vivekananda's Reflection

This single verse is perhaps the most quoted line in all of Hindu philosophy — and one of the most profound pieces of wisdom in human history. Krishna is not saying that results don't matter. He is saying that your attachment to results corrupts the purity of your action. When you work without grasping at outcomes, you work with your full being — and paradoxically, you tend to do your best work.

This is not passivity or indifference. It is the highest form of engagement: to give everything to the action itself, while releasing the need to control what comes from it. This is the Karma Yoga path — union with the divine through action.
