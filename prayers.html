The most essential prayer — recited before any new beginning, journey, or endeavor. Invoke Lord Ganesha's blessings to remove all obstacles.

Vakratunda Mahakaya

Shloka
वक्रतुण्ड महाकाय सूर्यकोटि समप्रभ।
निर्विघ्नं कुरु मे देव सर्वकार्येषु सर्वदा॥
Vakratunda Mahakaya Suryakoti Samaprabha
Nirvighnam Kuru Me Deva Sarvakaryeshu Sarvada
O Lord with the curved trunk and mighty body,
whose radiance equals ten million suns —
bless me so that I am free from all obstacles
in all that I do, always and forever.
Word by Word Meaning
Vakratunda — One with the curved trunk (वक्र = curved, तुण्ड = trunk)

Mahakaya — One with the great/mighty body (महा = great, काय = body)

Suryakoti Samaprabha — Whose brilliance equals ten million suns

Nirvighnam Kuru Me — Grant me freedom from obstacles (निर्विघ्नं = obstacle-free, कुरु = make, मे = for me)

Sarvakaryeshu Sarvada — In all my work, always
  • Recite before starting any new task, project, or journey
  • Traditionally the very first prayer of the day
  • Ganesha is the remover of obstacles (Vighnaharta) and the lord of new beginnings
  • Can be recited any number of times — even once with full devotion is sufficient

Morning Universal 3 min Vedic
▾
Gayatri Mantra — 108 Repetitions
Traditional recitation · 12 min
The Mantra
ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः
तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यं
भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि
धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात्॥
Om Bhur Bhuvah Svaha
Tat Savitur Varenyam
Bhargo Devasya Dhimahi
Dhiyo Yo Nah Prachodayat
Om — the primordial sound, the source of all creation
We meditate upon the divine light of that adorable Sun
May that supreme divine spirit illuminate our intellect
And inspire us toward righteous action and truth
The Deeper Meaning
The Gayatri is not merely a prayer — it is a meditation on light as consciousness. The "sun" (Savitru) here refers not just to the physical sun, but to the divine intelligence that animates all creation. When we recite this mantra, we are asking that same intelligence to light up our own minds.

The three worlds mentioned — Bhur (earth), Bhuvah (atmosphere), Svah (heavens) — represent the entirety of existence. This mantra transcends all divisions of caste, sect, or tradition. It belongs to every Hindu.
  • Recite facing east at sunrise for maximum benefit
  • Traditionally recited 108 times using a japa mala
  • Even 3, 9, or 27 repetitions with full attention carries great merit
  • The Gayatri is found in the Rigveda (3.62.10) — among the oldest prayers in human history

The great victory over death. One of the most powerful mantras in the Vedas — chanted for healing, protection, liberation, and overcoming fear.
Healing Protection Shiva Powerful
▾
Maha Mrityunjaya — Slow recitation
With pronunciation guide · 8 min
The Mantra
ॐ त्र्यम्बकं यजामहे
सुगन्धिं पुष्टिवर्धनम्।
उर्वारुकमिव बन्धनान्
मृत्योर्मुक्षीय माऽमृतात्॥
Om Tryambakam Yajamahe
Sugandhim Pushtivardhanam
Urvarukamiva Bandhanan
Mrityor Mukshiya Maamritat
We worship the three-eyed Lord Shiva,
who is fragrant and nourishes all beings.
As a cucumber is severed from its vine,
may we be liberated from death — not from immortality.
Significance & Practice
The Maha Mrityunjaya is called the "Great Death-Conquering Mantra." The three eyes of Shiva represent the sun, moon, and fire — past, present, and future. The beautiful metaphor of the cucumber (urvaruka) separating naturally from its vine speaks to a death that is peaceful and timely, not violent or premature.

This mantra is recited when someone is ill, facing danger, before surgery, during grief, or in times of deep fear. It is among the most requested mantras at hospitals in India.
  • Chant 108 times for full healing puja
  • Particularly powerful on Mondays and during Shivaratri
  • Can be recited for another person's wellbeing
  • Found in the Rigveda (7.59.12) and Yajurveda

Hanuman Chalisa — Full Recitation
With Hindi transliteration · 15 min
Doha 1 — Opening
श्रीगुरु चरन सरोज रज निज मनु मुकुरु सुधारि।
बरनउँ रघुबर बिमल जसु जो दायकु फल चारि॥
Shri Guru Charan Saroj Raj Nij Manu Mukuru Sudhari
Barnau Raghubar Bimal Jasu Jo Dayaku Phal Chari
Having cleansed the mirror of my mind with the dust of my Guru's lotus feet,
I narrate the pure glory of Raghuvara — which bestows the four fruits of life.
Doha 2
बुद्धिहीन तनु जानिके सुमिरौ पवन कुमार।
बल बुद्धि बिद्या देहु मोहिं हरहु कलेस बिकार॥
Buddhihin Tanu Janike Sumirau Pavan Kumar
Bal Buddhi Vidya Dehu Mohi Harahu Kales Bikar
Knowing myself to be without intelligence, I remember you, O Son of the Wind.
Grant me strength, wisdom, and knowledge — and remove all my afflictions and impurities.
Chaupai 1
जय हनुमान ज्ञान गुन सागर।
जय कपीस तिहुँ लोक उजागर॥
Jai Hanuman Gyan Gun Sagar
Jai Kapis Tihun Lok Ujagar
Victory to Hanuman, ocean of wisdom and virtue.
Victory to the Lord of monkeys, illuminator of the three worlds.
Chaupai 2
राम दूत अतुलित बल धामा।
अञ्जनि पुत्र पवनसुत नामा॥
Ram Doot Atulit Bal Dhama
Anjani Putra Pavansut Nama
Messenger of Rama, abode of incomparable strength,
Known as the son of Anjani and the son of the Wind.
About the Hanuman Chalisa
Written by the poet-saint Tulsidas in the 16th century in Awadhi Hindi, the Chalisa (meaning "forty") contains exactly 40 verses — 2 Dohas (couplets) and 38 Chaupais (quatrains). It is the single most recited Hindu prayer in the world, with an estimated one billion recitations daily across India alone.

Hanuman represents the perfect devotee — one who has completely surrendered their ego to God while possessing boundless strength, intelligence, and courage. His story in the Ramayana is one of absolute selfless service.
  • Recite on Tuesdays and Saturdays — Hanuman's sacred days
  • Chanting 11 times on a Tuesday is considered especially auspicious
  • The full Chalisa takes approximately 12–15 minutes to recite
  • Associated with removing fear, evil influences, and mental disturbances
  • Best recited in the morning, but beneficial any time

Shiva Panchakshara Stotram
शिव पञ्चाक्षर स्तोत्र
Five verses — each beginning with the five syllables of Na-Ma-Shi-Va-Ya. Composed by Adi Shankaracharya, the most luminous theological mind in Hindu history.
Monday Shankaracharya 5 min Sanskrit
▾
Shiva Panchakshara Stotram
Classical Sanskrit · 5 min
Verse 1 — Na (न)
नागेन्द्रहाराय त्रिलोचनाय
भस्माङ्गरागाय महेश्वराय।
नित्याय शुद्धाय दिगम्बराय
तस्मै नकाराय नमः शिवाय॥
Nagendra Haraya Trilochanaya
Bhashmanga Ragaya Maheshwaraya
Nityaya Shuddhaya Digambaraya
Tasmai Nakaraya Namah Shivaya
To the one who wears the serpent king as a garland, who has three eyes,
Who is adorned with ash, who is the great Lord,
Who is eternal, pure, and clothed in the directions (the sky itself) —
To that Shiva represented by the syllable "Na" — I bow.
The Five Syllables
Namasivaya (नमः शिवाय) — the Panchakshara, or five-syllable mantra — is considered the very heart of the Shaiva tradition. Each syllable corresponds to one of the five elements: Na (earth), Ma (water), Shi (fire), Va (air), Ya (ether/space). The mantra is thus a salutation to Shiva as the totality of creation itself.

Sri Mahalakshmi Ashtakam
श्री महालक्ष्मी अष्टकम्
Eight verses in praise of Goddess Lakshmi — the divine mother of abundance, beauty, and spiritual prosperity. Recited on Fridays and during Diwali.
Friday Diwali Prosperity Evening
▾
Mahalakshmi Ashtakam
Evening recitation · 7 min
Verse 1
नमस्तेस्तु महामाये श्रीपीठे सुरपूजिते।
शङ्खचक्रगदाहस्ते महालक्ष्मि नमोऽस्तुते॥
Namasteste Mahamaye Shripeethe Surapujite
Shankhachakra Gadahaste Mahalakshmi Namostute
Salutations to you, O Great Illusion, worshipped by the gods on the sacred seat,
Holding conch, discus, and mace in your hands — O Mahalakshmi, I bow to you.
When to Recite
The Mahalakshmi Ashtakam is traditionally recited on Friday evenings, ideally after lighting a ghee lamp before Lakshmi's image. During Diwali, it is recited as part of the main puja. Lakshmi represents not just material wealth but all forms of abundance — health, relationships, wisdom, and spiritual prosperity.
  • Best recited on Fridays — Lakshmi's sacred day
  • Light a yellow or white ghee lamp while reciting
  • Offer lotus flowers or marigolds if available
  • Particularly powerful during Diwali puja

Pratah Smarana — Morning Remembrance
प्रातः स्मरण
The first prayer of the day — recited before getting out of bed. Three brief verses to sanctify the hands, the earth, and the mind at the very start of morning.
First of Day 1 min Universal Daily
▾
Pratah Smarana — Morning Prayers
3 verses · 2 min
Before Rising — Look at Your Palms
कराग्रे वसते लक्ष्मीः करमध्ये सरस्वती।
करमूले तु गोविन्दः प्रभाते करदर्शनम्॥
Karagre Vasate Lakshmi Karamadhye Saraswati
Karamule Tu Govinda Prabhate Karadarshanam
At the tip of my hands dwells Lakshmi (goddess of prosperity),
In the middle of my palms dwells Saraswati (goddess of wisdom),
At the base of my hands dwells Govinda (Lord Vishnu) —
Thus I behold my hands every morning.
Before Touching the Ground
समुद्रवसने देवि पर्वतस्तनमण्डले।
विष्णुपत्नि नमस्तुभ्यं पादस्पर्शं क्षमस्व मे॥
Samudravasane Devi Parvatastana Mandale
Vishnupatni Namastubhyam Padasparsham Kshamasva Me
O Divine Mother Earth, dressed in oceans, with mountains as your bosom,
O consort of Vishnu — I bow to you.
Please forgive me for touching you with my feet.
The Wisdom of This Practice
These two verses are perhaps the most profound daily practice in all of Hinduism — and the simplest. Before a single step is taken, before checking your phone, before thinking about the day ahead — you pause to see the divine in your own hands, and to ask forgiveness from the Earth before placing your weight on her.

This is the essence of Hindu spirituality: the sacred is not in a distant temple — it is in your palms, it is in the ground beneath your feet, it is everywhere, always.

Saraswati Vandana
Sanskrit with Hindi meaning · 4 min
Shloka
या कुन्देन्दुतुषारहारधवला या शुभ्रवस्त्रावृता
या वीणावरदण्डमण्डितकरा या श्वेतपद्मासना।
या ब्रह्माच्युतशंकरप्रभृतिभिर्देवैः सदा वन्दिता
सा मां पातु सरस्वती भगवती निःशेषजाड्यापहा॥
Ya Kundendutushara Haradhavala Ya Shubhravastravrita
Ya Vinavara Dandamandita Kara Ya Shvetapadmasana
Ya Brahmachyuta Shankara Prabhritibhir Devaih Sada Vandita
Sa Mam Patu Saraswati Bhagavati Nihshesha Jadyapaha
She who is as pure white as the jasmine flower, the moon, or frost —
Who is robed in white garments, whose hands are adorned with the veena,
Who sits upon a white lotus, eternally worshipped by Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva —
May that Goddess Saraswati protect me, and remove all dullness from my mind.
